Research Methods and Professional Practice

This module provides a practical foundation in research methods that enables students to conduct research in practical settings. The approach covers various tools and techniques that a researcher may deploy and facilitates continual feedback throughout the module through cases and project tasks.

Module Objectives

  • Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural and ethical issues that affect computing professionals
  • Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the applicable computing field.
  • Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.
  • Produce and evaluate critically the resulting research proposal for the chosen topic.

    5. Interviews and Survey Methods

    • Interview methods and survey methods.
    • Population and sample.
    • Pre- and post-testing.

    Unit Reflection & Artefacts

    This unit focused on interviews, surveys, and questionnaires. I learned that there is so much more involved when it comes to these data collection techniques. My initial ideas for these data collection methods were that you decide on the information that you require, create the questionnaire, and collect and report on the findings. This is not the case and is not as simple as the average person will think. Much is involved in creating questionnaires and surveys, and some important aspects need to be taken into consideration. I realized that it’s important that surveys and questionnaires need to be ethically conducted, they should not contain any bias in them as this could negatively impact the outcome of the results. Surveys also need to protect the respondents and not cause any harm, protect, and anonymize personal information, be transparent, and obtain consent from the respondents (Barrow et al., 2022) (Kaiser, 2009) (Fisher, 2020). This was further highlighted in the case study that was part of a reflective activity on the inappropriate use of surveys conducted by Cambridge Analytica using Facebook (Confessore, 2018). This case study was very helpful in deducing the importance of surveys and questionnaires and the colossal impact they have on research. This will provide much guidance in my future capstone project and has made me aware of the importance of designing a survey in a certain way and in an ethical manner and prioritizing the interests of the respondents. References Barrow, J., Brannan, G., & Khandhar, P. (2022) Research Ethics. StatPearls Publishing. [online] Available from: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK459281/ [Accessed 26 March 2023]. Confessore, N. (2018) Cambridge Analytica and Facebook: The Scandal and the Fallout So Far. The New York Times. Fisher, S. (2020) Ethical issues to consider when conducting survey research [online]. Available from: https://www.qualtrics.com/blog/ethical-issues-for-online-surveys [Accessed March 26 2023]. Kaiser, K. (2009) Protecting respondent confidentiality in qualitative research. Qualitative health research.
